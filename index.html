Since 1871

A Living Tradition in Rural Quebec

For over 150 years, the Havelock Fair has brought together families, farmers, and friends in the heart of the Haut-Saint-Laurent region. What began as a small agricultural gathering in 1871 has grown into one of Canada's most cherished community events.

From livestock shows and 4-H competitions to antique tractors, live country music, maple treats, and children's games — there's something for everyone at the Havelock Fairgrounds.

455 Route 202, Havelock, Quebec J0S 2C0 Minutes from the Canada–US border

What to Expect

🐎

Livestock & 4-H

Heavy horses, cattle shows, and youth competitions showcasing the best of rural agriculture.

🎵

Live Music

Country, folk, and local talent performing all day on our outdoor stage.

🚜

Antique Tractors

The Chateauguay Valley Garden Tractor Club and vintage machinery on display.

🌽

Local Food & Treats

Maple products, BBQ, homemade pies, and farm-fresh flavors from local producers.

🎠

Kids' Activities

Games, face painting, petting zoo, and hands-on farm experiences for the little ones.

🧵

Arts & Crafts

Handmade goods, quilts, woodworking, and artisan vendors from across the region.

Admission & Information

Everything you need to know before you arrive

🎟️

Adults

General admission covers both days of the fair. Children 12 and under free.

🕗

Hours

Gates open 8:00 AM both days. Exhibitors: 6:00 AM Saturday, 7:00 AM Sunday. Closes 5:00 PM.

🍗

Food & Drinks

BBQ chicken dinner, canteen, sugar shanty, and local vendors. No outside food restrictions.

🅿️

Parking

Free parking on-site. Follow volunteer attendants. Overflow parking available along Route 202.
