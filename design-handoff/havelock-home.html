Chapter One
The Gathering Field

Since 1871, one field in the Haut-Saint-Laurent has done the same quiet, extraordinary thing every September — it gathers a community. Farmers and families, competitors and first-time visitors, drawn together by the oldest rhythm there is: the harvest.

What began as a modest agricultural gathering is now the 155th edition — one of Canada's most enduring rural traditions, and a place where heritage is not remembered but lived.

Heavy horses in the morning mist. The Chateauguay Valley tractors polished to a shine. The same maple, the same music, the same welcome — carried forward, year after year, by the people who call this place home.

Plan Your Visit

September 12 & 13, 2026

Gates open at 8:00 AM both days at the Havelock Fairgrounds — 455 Route 202, minutes from the Canada–US border. Children 12 and under are always free.

Admission

General admission covers both days. Children 12 & under free.

Hours

Gates 8:00 AM both days. Closes 5:00 PM.

Food & Drink

BBQ chicken dinner, canteen, sugar shanty & local vendors.

Parking

Free on-site parking. Overflow along Route 202.
